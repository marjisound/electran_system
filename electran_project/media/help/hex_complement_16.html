The ns-Complement representation of negative numbers

In the lectures, we developed from the basic addition and subtraction algorithms, a means of converting any number into its negative.

Given a number in base b with symbols tn tn-1 tn-2 .. t2 t1 t0

We convert each ti into si with the formula

si = b-1-ti

To give the number in base b with symbols snsn-1sn-2 .. s2s1s0 and then we add 1.

This is the negative of the number in Ns-complement form.

Example in decimal: produce negative of 73510 = 000073510

b = 10 : sn = b - 1 - tn = 10 - 1 - tn = 9- tn
Convert ti to si: 9-0 = 0: 9-7=2 : 9-5 = 4
-00073510 = ...999973510 + 1 = ..99973610

Negative (complement form) of 73510 is .973510

Negative of 12345616 is FFEDCBAA16

By same method using b = 1610 : sn = b - 1 - tn = 1016 - 1 - tn = F16- tn
