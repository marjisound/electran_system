Help page for Hexadecimal-binary conversion

Hexadecimal Numbers

Everything in computers is groups of bits. It is tedious to write, difficult to read and analyse patterns of bits, so it is useful to have a short form to express bit patterns. Hexadecimal numbers are generally now used to do this. There are 16 of these numbers, each of which can be mapped to a 4-bit binary pattern as in the table.

Relationships between Hexadecimal symbols and 4-bit binary patterns
Hexadecimal Binary Hexadecimal Binary
0 0000 8 1000
1 0001 9 1001
2 0010 A or a 1010
3 0011 B or b 1011
4 0100 C or c 1100
5 0101 D or d 1101
6 0110 E or e 1110
7 0111 F or f 1111

Any binary pattern can be broken into groups of 4 and mapped into hexadecimal numbers. The reverse operation is equally trivial. Of course where the number of bits is not a multiple of 4 it is necessary to indicate the number of bits and know from which end of the bit pattern the grouping is done. However, modern computer systems usually have all bit patterns at multiple of 8, so that this problem rarely arises. Always group binary numbers into groups of 4 bits starting from the right

A last example, showing what to when the number of binary digits is not a multiple of 4
Binary 10 0101 1110 1111
hex 2AEF
When converting from hexadecimal binary you can spaces between your bits to make it easier to check what you have written - the answer checker will throw away all spaces. When converting from binary to hex you can use either upper of lower case for letters.
